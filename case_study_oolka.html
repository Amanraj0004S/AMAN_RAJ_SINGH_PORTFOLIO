Oolka raised ₹130Cr and built an AI agent for consumer credit improvement in India. 6 million users. $2.5M ARR. So why are users calling it a paid ChatGPT?

01 · Company Context

The bet
Oolka made.

Oolka is building what no one else is attempting at scale in India — an AI agent that doesn't just show you your credit score, but actively works to improve it. Their acquisition wedge is smart: offer a free credit score check, catch users at peak financial anxiety, convert that moment into an app download. The differentiated promise over CIBIL, Paisabazaar, and CRED is clear. Those platforms stop at the number. Oolka promises to change it.

02 · User Evidence

100 reviews.
4 patterns.

I analysed 100 recent Play Store reviews (May 6–10, 2026). 12 were 1–2 star. But those 12 said the same things repeatedly. I then pulled my full negative review sample — 20 users across 1 and 2 star ratings — and clustered every complaint. Four patterns emerged with striking consistency.

Transparency note: The cluster counts (out of 20) are from the full negative review sample collected across 1 and 2 star ratings. The 12/100 figure is from the most recent 100 reviews only (May 6–10, 2026). Both sets are from Google Play Store exclusively.

8/20
AI Reliability
Users say the AI is generic, incomplete, and no better than a free LLM. It fails to deliver on the core product promise of personalised credit guidance.
"You can download your credit report and use free AI tools like Claude — however, in my experience, the AI does not seem mature or reliable. Instead of offering practical guidance, it mostly functions like a basic AI tool that generates generic information and simple email drafts."
7/20
Trust & Accuracy
Users report score mismatches between Oolka, CIBIL, and Paytm. Wrong numbers in a high-stakes financial context destroy trust immediately.
"My CIBIL score is 718 but this app is showing less than 600 and telling me to take their subscription for increasing my score."
7/20
Monetisation Friction
Paywall appears before users experience any value. The product asks for money before demonstrating what money buys — a classic activation failure.
"You can also take membership by paying Rs.99 and after that there will be zero in the name of service. These people get all the work done through chatbots — how can I get the change in CIBIL?"
5/20
Data & Reporting Gap
Oolka works only on Experian data but advertises CIBIL improvement. Most Indian banks use CIBIL for loan approvals. This is a promise-delivery mismatch baked into the product.
"It only shows an Experian report, which is useful, but doesn't include CIBIL — the most important score used by banks in India. Most banks in India rely mainly on the CIBIL report for loan approvals."
What works
Loan closure identification — users find and close useless loans
Credit card closure process — smooth, fast, appreciated
Identifying instruments dragging the score down
What doesn't
Long-term credit improvement — no structured plan
Personalised AI guidance — feels generic and reactive
Trust at onboarding — score mismatch kills confidence early
Value before money — paywall appears before proof of concept
03 · Problem Statement

One sentence
that explains
the churn.

Synthesised Problem Statement

A user with a low credit score comes to Oolka wanting a personalised credit improvement plan — they get asked for payment before receiving vague AI responses with no clear roadmap and no structured execution, just a paid ChatGPT.

This costs Oolka user trust and fast dissatisfaction, leading to negative word of mouth and the loss of long-term recurring revenue — the very foundation their ARR depends on.

04 · Root Cause · 5 Whys

Asking why
five times.

Why 1
Users are churning before seeing value from the product.
Why 2
Because they hit a paywall before the free version shows them anything meaningful — all utility is gated behind payment.
Why 3
But even the premium version doesn't deliver a structured, personalised improvement plan — it delivers generic responses.
Why 4
Because the AI only handles simple tasks — basic email drafts, instrument closures — not complex strategies like loan planning, spending behaviour analysis, or score trajectory forecasting.
Why 5
Because the AI is reactive, not proactive. It waits for what the user types instead of cross-questioning the user, analysing the full credit profile holistically, and generating a forward-looking personalised roadmap on its own.
"Oolka's AI is a reactive chatbot, not an intelligent credit advisor. It processes what users type — not what users need."
05 · Solution Framework

Four interventions,
one direction.

1 Priority
Core Product Redesign
From Reactive Chatbot to Proactive Credit Advisor
Train the AI on historical credit profile patterns so it stops waiting for user inputs and starts leading. First interaction: user submits structured data — score, active loans, credit card usage, payment history gaps — and the AI generates a personalised 3-week credit improvement plan. It identifies hidden score drains, surfaces quick wins, and updates the plan week on week as behaviour changes. The AI asks questions. It doesn't wait for them.
Solves for: 8/20 users — AI feels generic, no better than free tools
2 Priority
Activation Model Redesign
Value Before Paywall
The 3-week personalised plan is shown in full before any subscription is asked. The user sees exactly what Oolka found about their credit profile and what it recommends — for free, once. Only after this moment of demonstrated value does the paywall appear, offering monthly or annual plans to unlock automated execution. The logic: if you show someone their own financial blind spots for the first time, paying ₹99 to fix them is an easy decision. Cost to Oolka: more tokens per free user. Benefit: trust before the ask.
Solves for: 7/20 users — paywall appeared before a single useful insight
3 Priority
Premium Tier Redesign
Automate the Execution Layer
The paid tier stops being about advice and becomes about action. It suggests the best loan closure options ranked by credit score impact, rates the user's credit card spending behaviour against their improvement goals, initiates dispute letters, and completes tasks with user confirmation at key steps. Execution — not information — becomes the USP. The product graduates from telling users what to do to doing it with them.
Solves for: 7/20 users — paid and got nothing executed
4 Priority
Quick Win · Zero Engineering
Transparency Fix for the Bureau Gap
Oolka cannot fix their Experian-only data contract — that is a business decision, not a product one. But they can stop surprising users with it. One line on the onboarding screen: "Oolka works with Experian data. Your score here may differ from your CIBIL score." That single sentence — zero engineering effort — eliminates the score mismatch complaint before it forms. Transparency is a product decision even when the underlying constraint is not.
Solves for: 5/20 users — score mismatch between Oolka, CIBIL, and Paytm
06 · Success Metrics

How you know
it's working.

Solution 1
Plan Adherence Rate
% of users who complete at least 2 out of 3 weekly actions suggested by the AI plan. Measured at end of Week 3 for each new user cohort.
Target: 40%+ adherence by Week 3. If users follow the plan, the AI is personalised enough to feel actionable — not generic.
Solution 2
Free-to-Paid Conversion Rate
% of users who view the full 3-week plan and purchase a subscription within 7 days. Baseline: industry freemium fintech average is 3–5%.
Target: 8%+ — the value moment of seeing your own blind spots justifies a premium above market average.
Solution 3
Execution Feature Retention Rate
% of paid users who return to use the execution layer — loan suggestions, spending rating, dispute initiation — at least once per week after Month 1.
Target: 60%+ weekly active paid users. Repeat usage and permission grants signal genuine utility, not novelty.
Solution 4
Bureau Mismatch Complaint Rate
% of 1–2 star reviews mentioning score discrepancy between Oolka and CIBIL or Paytm. Current baseline: 5 out of every 20 negative reviewers.
Target: drop to under 1/20 within 60 days of adding the transparency line on onboarding.

"A reactive AI is not a product — it is a mirror with a subscription fee. The moment Oolka starts leading the conversation instead of waiting for it, the churn stops."
