Case Study 04 · May 2026 · YC W26

Doomersion The hook that forgot to land.

A YC-backed language learning app built a genius acquisition hook — learn by doomscrolling. Then forgot to teach users how to use it. This is the activation gap hiding inside a brilliant idea.

CompanyDoomersion · Doomlingo Inc.
StageYC W26 · 4-person team
ResearchPrimary usage + App Store reviews
FrameworksJTBD · KANO · ICE · 5 Whys
04
01 · Company Context

The bet
Doomersion made.

Doomersion's thesis is simple and sharp: people already spend hours doomscrolling. Instead of fighting that habit, route it into language learning. Short videos in your target language, matched to your level, getting harder as you scroll. The acquisition hook is genuinely differentiated — not another streak-based app, not another flashcard system. A feed.

0→1
Product Stage
Must Haves not yet solid. Activation is the core unsolved problem.

JTBD: Users aren't hiring Doomersion to "learn a language." They're hiring it to make their screen time feel productive — without changing the habit. That's a different job entirely. One leads to Duolingo. The other leads to something nobody has built yet.

Competitive positioning: Duolingo owns structured learning. YouTube owns long-form immersion. TikTok owns attention. Doomersion is attempting the gap between TikTok and Duolingo — contextual learning inside the scroll. No one has cracked this at scale.

02 · User Research & Evidence

What users
actually experienced.

Research transparency: Google Play Store — 0 reviews (10K+ downloads, no written reviews yet). App Store — 47 ratings, 5 written reviews across Feb–Apr 2026. Primary research — 20 minutes of personal app usage, documented in real time. Reddit, Product Hunt, G2 — no relevant results found. Sample is small; this is an early-stage product with limited public data. Primary usage findings and App Store reviews are treated equally as evidence.

Primary user segment identified: Gen Z college student, 18–25, heavy Instagram/TikTok user, wants their screen time to mean something — without it feeling like studying. Evidence: app name "Doomersion," founder's background in gaming, App Store review language, product positioning.

P1 bug
Must Have
Performance & Technical Bugs
App lag kills the core TikTok-like experience. Arabic transcripts render as one long word. Rewind crashes video. Double-tap to like broken. Captions delayed. The technical foundation undermines the product's core promise.
"The app is pretty slow and doesn't always load videos in a timely manner, making the point of the app diminished slightly."
MH core
Must Have
Content Language Mismatch
Users who declare Chinese as their target language receive videos dominated by Spanish on-screen text. The entertainment context requires knowing one foreign language; the learning requires knowing another. Both are inaccessible simultaneously.
"Sometimes you'll get content in a diff language or the subtitles don't correctly work or have weird explanations."
P2 feed
Performance
Content Variety Too Narrow
Feed currently shows only skits, comedy, and dance videos. Users want search, algorithm influence, and broader content types. For less common languages the problem is amplified — the feed feels repetitive and boring fast.
"Right now it seems to only show skits, comedy, and some dance videos and it would be nice if there was either a search bar to find videos I enjoy to influence my algorithm."
MH ux
Must Have
Broken Onboarding & Lost Users
No guidance on how the core loop works. Users see 4 videos before a signup wall with no understanding of the product's mechanism. The clickable word below the video has no visible contextual bridge to the video. Users feel lost.
Personal research: "I did not get what it is or what and how to use which made me think of uninstalling it."
What works
Acquisition hook — "learn by doomscrolling" is genuinely differentiated positioning
Word tap feature — when discovered, clicking a word and seeing breakdown works well
Quiz feature — word quizzes are appreciated by users who reach them
Core concept — power users report 3-hour sessions vs Duolingo's 5-minute average
What doesn't
Activation — users don't understand the core loop without guidance
Content matching — wrong language videos shown to declared target learners
AI feature — 3 consecutive errors in personal testing; no evidence it works reliably
Performance — lag on a short-form video app is fatal to the core experience
03 · Problem Definition

One sentence
explains the churn.

Synthesised Problem Statement

A Gen Z user, tired of mindless doomscrolling on Instagram or TikTok, hired Doomersion to make their screen time feel productive — learning a language without changing the habit. Instead they encountered a broken first experience: no guidance on how the app works, videos with content in unrelated languages, lag that kills the feed, and a UI that demands too much cognitive effort to follow.

This costs Doomersion the one thing a 4-person YC startup cannot afford to lose — Day 1 to Day 7 retention — leaving a brilliant acquisition hook with no activation to back it up.

Why 1
Users churn before understanding the product's value.
Why 2
Because the first experience is confusing — lag, no onboarding guidance, and videos in unrelated languages make the core loop invisible.
Why 3
Because the feed serves content that doesn't match the user's declared target language, breaking context before learning can begin.
Why 4
Because the content curation algorithm pulls videos based on the target language word appearing somewhere in the video — not based on whether the video itself is in or about that language.
Why 5
Because the app has no backend audio processing to understand what language is actually being spoken — it relies on surface-level tagging. Proactive audio segmentation and contextual subtitle generation were not prioritised in the 0→1 build.
"Doomersion's algorithm places words in context but not videos in context. It finds the right word but puts it inside the wrong world — leaving the user linguistically lost before the learning even starts."
04 · KANO Prioritisation

What to fix first
and why.

Doomersion is a 10-week old product with a 4-person team. KANO principle: fix all Must Haves before any Performance feature. No Delighters until the foundation is solid.

Must Have — Fix First
Feed Language Intelligence — wrong language content kills trust before learning starts
Progressive Onboarding — without guidance activation fails entirely
Context-Aware AI — broken AI destroys the second core promise
Performance — Fix After
Lag & Video Quality — users compare to TikTok; lag is fatal but not the first reason they leave
Feed Personalisation — builds long-term habit but requires Must Haves working first
Delighters — Later
Social features — cohort learning, leaderboards
Corporate-standard certifications — validated learning credentials
Creator tools — let native speakers contribute content
05 · Solution Framework

Five interventions,
one direction.

1
Must HaveCore Algorithm
Feed Language Intelligence
User Story: As a college student who spends hours on Instagram Reels, I want to absorb a new language through videos of real people from that culture, so that my screen time feels productive without feeling like structured study.
Backend AI processes every video before it reaches the feed — detecting spoken language through audio analysis and visual cues. Only videos matching the user's declared target language enter the feed. For matched videos, real-time sentence-level subtitles appear below in the target language. Tapping any sentence pauses the video and shows word-by-word translation with context.
Constraint
Backend processing increases feed load latency. Requires powerful compute infrastructure — paid cloud processing and model training costs significant for a 4-person team.
Trade-off
Choosing server-side language filtering over on-device processing — more accurate but adds latency. Speed is sacrificed for reliability at this stage.
A/B Test
50% new users get current feed vs 50% get language-filtered feed. Measure: Day 1 activation rate. Success: 20%+ improvement in filtered group after 500 users per group.
2
Must HaveQuick Win
Progressive Onboarding
User Story: As a college student used to the effortless smoothness of Instagram Reels, I want to start learning a new language without any setup confusion, so that my free time feels enhanced — not spent figuring out a new app.
After initial questions, user lands on feed with a guided first experience. Nudge 1: tap the subtitle word. Nudge 2: swipe up/down. After 2–3 videos: short Gen Z-style prompt to try the AI feature. After AI used: 2 more free scrolls then signup wall. Only these steps — no clutter, no information overload. Main feed visible in background throughout so the user experiences the product while learning it.
Constraint
Risk is purely in nudge design — intrusive nudges backfire. Requires careful UX testing to find the right weight: visible enough to guide, light enough to not annoy.
Trade-off
Choosing linear guided onboarding over interactive tutorial — fewer steps means less drop-off, even though some features remain undiscovered initially.
A/B Test
50% get current experience vs 50% get guided onboarding. Measure: Day 3 retention rate. Success: 15%+ higher return rate in guided group. Fastest test to conclude — 72 hours per user.
3
Must HaveAI Feature
Context-Aware AI
User Story: As a college student used to asking doubts fast using LLMs like ChatGPT, I want AI language support seamlessly inside the app, so that I don't have to switch to ChatGPT and break my attention.
AI tracks which part of the app it was opened from and understands user intent from context. Instead of generic errors, it delivers short constructive redirects — tells the user specifically what to try instead of hitting a dead end. When the AI genuinely can't process a request, it responds with a specific, useful suggestion rather than "Sorry, I had trouble responding."
Constraint
Every AI call needs to carry session context — current screen, user intent, recent actions — increasing token usage per request significantly.
Trade-off
Choosing context-aware short responses over a full conversational tutor — reliability matters more than depth. A simple AI that always responds beats a smart AI that errors out.
A/B Test
50% get current AI vs 50% get context-aware AI. Measure: AI feature engagement rate per session. Secondary: session length. Success: 25%+ higher AI usage in new group.
4
PerformanceInfrastructure
Performance & Lag Optimisation
User Story: As a college student used to instant-loading feeds, I want a smooth experience without waiting or frustration, so that my learning time feels effortless and I don't switch back to Instagram or TikTok.
Declutter feed UI to reduce rendering load. Implement adaptive bitrate streaming — video quality adjusts dynamically to available bandwidth so users on 3G don't experience lag. Apply AI-assisted video smoothing capped at a defined pixel threshold, preserving original content integrity while improving perceived quality on lower-end devices.
Constraint
AI video smoothing at scale multiplies compute costs significantly across concurrent users. Adaptive bitrate streaming is the more cost-effective alternative at this team size.
Trade-off
Choosing adaptive bitrate streaming over AI smoothing — proven technology, lower cost, doesn't multiply per-user compute costs at scale.
A/B Test
50% current delivery vs 50% optimised adaptive bitrate. Measure: Day 1 churn rate. Segment specifically by lower-end Android devices and 3G connections for sharpest signal.
5
PerformanceLong-Term Retention
Personalised Feed Algorithm
User Story: As a college student with a highly personalised Instagram and YouTube feed, I want my learning feed to understand my viewing patterns and show varied content, so that the app feels intelligent and relevant — not generic and outdated.
Dynamic recommendation engine tracks watch time per video in real time. If a user spends more time on a content type — identified through subtitle keywords and video hashtags — push similar content. If engagement drops across 3–4 consecutive videos, reset to general feed and recalculate. Force 7–8 general videos periodically even when engagement is strong, to prevent filter bubble fatigue. Learns within session first, then across sessions.
Constraint
Algorithm development time is significant. Edge cases are complex — passive scrollers, mixed intent users, cold start problem for new users with no watch history.
Trade-off
Choosing watch-time as primary signal over multi-signal algorithm — simplest reliable signal available. Hashtags and subtitles add complexity a small team can't maintain cleanly in v1.
A/B Test
50% static feed vs 50% dynamic watch-time algorithm. Measure: Day 7 and Day 30 retention. Longest test window — 30 days minimum. Run in parallel with faster tests.

08 · Risks & Mitigations

What could
go wrong.

MH1
Feed Load Latency
Backend processing of every video increases the time before content reaches the user's feed — on a short-form video app, any perceptible load time breaks the scroll experience.
Mitigation
Pre-filter content on first user profiling to build a cached content library. Give the real-time system a head start so the user never feels the processing delay.
MH2
Onboarding Drop-off
Too many steps before the main screen causes the user to feel onboarding is the product — they came to scroll, not to be taught how to scroll.
Mitigation
Show the main feed in the background between nudge steps. User experiences the product while learning it — service delivery and education happen simultaneously.
MH3
Token Cost Multiplication
Context-aware AI requires passing session state with every API call — current screen, user intent, recent actions — significantly increasing token usage and backend cost per user.
Mitigation
Limit AI access to specific touchpoints in the app. Shorter context window per call keeps costs controlled without sacrificing the contextual intelligence.
P1
Content Integrity Compromise
AI video smoothing risks altering the original video content — over-smoothed footage loses authenticity, which matters particularly for cultural and language immersion content.
Mitigation
Backend caps smoothing at a defined pixel threshold. Preserves original content integrity while improving perceived quality. Never apply above the cap regardless of device quality.
P2
Filter Bubble Fatigue
Watch-time algorithm creates a content echo chamber — users get locked into one content type even when engagement is strong, eventually exhausting that category and churning faster than a varied feed would cause.
Mitigation
Force 7–8 general videos into the feed periodically even when the engagement trend is positive. This resets and realigns user interest, preventing the filter bubble from forming while maintaining personalisation momentum.
09 · Vision & Roadmap

Where Doomersion
could go.

Now
0–3 mo
Fix the Foundation
Ship all three Must Haves. Progressive onboarding first — days to build, immediate retention impact. Language feed filtering and context-aware AI in parallel. Performance optimisation running alongside.
Next
3–6 mo

Ship the personalised feed algorithm. Introduce streak mechanics. Add vocabulary tracking across sessions. Build the repeat-usage trigger that turns curious downloaders into daily learners.

Alex is 21. Final year engineering in Pune. His Instagram feed knows him better than his friends do — travel reels, tech memes, Japanese street food videos he can't stop watching.

One night he sees a post about Doomersion. "Learn Japanese by doomscrolling." He downloads it at 11pm thinking he'll try it for five minutes.

The app asks him three questions. Target language. Level. Done. The feed opens — and a small nudge appears at the bottom. Not a tutorial. Just one instruction: "Tap any word below the video." He taps. The video pauses. The word breaks apart — kanji, reading, meaning. He didn't feel like he studied. He felt like he caught something.

Two videos later another nudge. "Swipe left to ask about what you just watched." He swipes. The AI asks him — "Did you catch what she said in that scene?" He types back. It responds in two lines. Specific. Fast. He didn't open ChatGPT. He didn't break his scroll.

Three weeks later Alex is 40 videos deep into his Japanese streak. His feed has figured him out — street food videos, anime reactions, Tokyo vlogs. All Japanese. All at his level. Getting harder without him noticing.

Then something new appears. A badge. "Conversational Beginner — Doomersion Verified." A short test. 10 videos. 10 word questions. He passes. He puts it on his LinkedIn.

His friend messages him: "bro what is this." He sends the download link.
